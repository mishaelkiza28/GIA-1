Our Story

Golden Investment Association

The Golden Investment Association (GIA) is a member-owned savings and investment group based in Adebe Cell, Western Ward, Kamdini Town Council, Oyam District, Uganda. Founded in 2025, GIA unites members under a shared commitment to financial discipline, collective action, and long-term wealth creation.

Operating under the motto "Make Your Money Work Harder" and the tagline "Invest Smart, Live Well," GIA provides a structured, transparent platform through which members save, access affordable credit, and participate in group investments.

Our Vision

To build sustainable wealth for members through long-term savings and strategic investments that create lasting financial security and prosperity.

Our Mission

To empower members through joint investment initiatives, financial literacy, and collective action for long-term financial prosperity while maintaining the highest standards of transparency and accountability.

Our Objectives

  • Promote collective savings as a foundation for wealth creation
  • Provide affordable credit and financial support to members
  • Facilitate group investment opportunities for capital growth

Organization

Governance Structure

GIA is governed by a democratic, member-driven structure that ensures transparency and accountability at all levels.

General Assembly

The General Assembly is the supreme authority comprising all registered members. It meets regularly to make major decisions including:

  • Approval of annual budgets and financial reports
  • Setting savings contribution rates and loan terms
  • Electing the Executive Committee
  • Approving group investment decisions
  • Amending the Constitution and By-Laws

Executive Committee

An elected Executive Committee manages day-to-day affairs and implements decisions made by the General Assembly. The Committee consists of:

  • Chairperson — Leads meetings and represents the association
  • Vice Chairperson — Supports the Chairperson and assumes duties as needed
  • Secretary — Maintains records and manages communications
  • Treasurer — Manages finances and reports on financial matters
  • Additional Members — Elected to provide additional oversight and expertise

Independent Oversight

To ensure accountability and accuracy:

  • Legal Advisor — Provides legal guidance and ensures compliance with laws and regulations
  • External Auditor — Conducts independent audits of financial records and practices
Democratic Decision-Making: Major investment decisions require a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote from the General Assembly, ensuring broad member consensus and protection of minority interests.
